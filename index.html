OSI Model — "How Data Travels" Map
Full FormOpen Systems Interconnection Model.
What it isA 7-layer framework describing how a message moves from a human tapping a screen to a signal traveling through a wire.
Why it's neededHelps police identify where a crime occurred — was it a physical break-in (Layer 1) or a hacked WhatsApp account (Layer 7)?
General LocationA conceptual "rulebook" used by every computer in the world.
Investigation LocationWhen you seize a device, look at Layer 7 logs — Application logs like WhatsApp, Instagram.

OSI Model — "How Data Travels" Map
How to read itLayer 1 is the "Ground" (Cables) and Layer 7 is the "Top" (The App).
Data Link — Layer 2This is where the MAC Address lives; it connects the hardware to the local network.

The AnalogyThink of it like a courier service. Layer 1 is the road, Layer 3 is the address on the box, and Layer 7 is the letter inside.

IP Address — "Digital House Number"
Full FormInternet Protocol Address.
What it isA unique string of numbers assigned to every device on a network.
Why it's neededIt is the first clue in any cybercrime. It tells you which internet connection was used to commit the act.
General LocationFound in your phone's "Network Settings" or by searching "What is my IP" on Google.
Investigation LocationFound in the server logs of the victim — e.g. the IP address that logged into a stolen bank account.

IPv4 AppearanceFour sets of numbers separated by dots — e.g. 103.45.2.11.

Static vs. Dynamic IPs — "The Changing Address"
What it isStatic means the IP never changes. Dynamic means the IP changes every time the user restarts their phone or router.
Why it's neededTo understand why an IP address you found yesterday might belong to a different person today.
The Indian ContextMost mobile users in India (JIO, Airtel) use Dynamic IPs to save resources.
Investigation ChallengeIf you track a Dynamic IP without a timestamp, you might arrest the wrong person.
Static Use CaseUsually used by big companies, banks, or police headquarters so their servers are always easy to find.

MAC Address — "Hardware Fingerprint"
How it looksSix pairs of characters — e.g. 00:0A:95:9D:68:16.
NICNetwork Interface Card — the physical part inside the phone that has the MAC address.

IMEI Full FormInternational Mobile Equipment Identity.
IMSI Full FormInternational Mobile Subscriber Identity.

Cell Tower Triangulation — "The Search Area"
What it isUsing the signal strength from 3 or more towers to calculate a suspect's location.
Why it's neededTo find a suspect's live location even if they have turned off GPS/Location services.
How it worksThe phone is always "talking" to the nearest towers to stay connected.
AccuracyIn cities (many towers), it is very accurate — within 50–100 metres. In villages, it may only be within 2–3 kilometres.
General LocationOnly accessible by the Telecom Service Provider (TSP).

VPN / Proxy Mechanics — "The Mask"
Full FormVirtual Private Network.
What it isAn app that creates an encrypted "tunnel." It hides your real Indian IP and replaces it with a foreign IP — e.g. USA.
Why it's neededTo understand why a "Threat Mail" appears to come from New York when the suspect is in Mumbai.
Proxy vs. VPNA Proxy only hides your IP for one app (like a browser). A VPN hides everything on the phone.
The "Middle-Man"The data goes: Suspect → VPN Server → Internet. The internet only sees the VPN Server's IP.

VPN / Proxy Mechanics — "The Mask"
LogsMost VPNs claim a "No-Logs" policy, making it very hard for police to get the real IP.

What it isThe "Phonebook of the Internet." It translates human names (google.com) into computer numbers (142.250.190.46).
Why it's neededIf you have a scam website name, DNS records tell you which IP address it is currently pointing to.
A-RecordThe most common record; it maps the domain name directly to an IPv4 address.
CNAMEAn alias record. It shows if a URL is actually just a mask for another hidden website.
MX-RecordShows the "Mail Exchange" server — tells you which service the criminal is using to send emails.

What it isMaking an email look like it came from "bank@sbi.com" when it actually came from a scammer.
Full FormSMTP — Simple Mail Transfer Protocol.
Why it's neededTo find the Real Sender of a fake or threatening email.
Email HeaderThe hidden part of an email. In Gmail, click "Three Dots" → "Show Original."
Received-FromThis line in the header shows the Real IP Address of the computer that sent the mail.

Email Spoofing & SMTP Logs
X-MailerShows what software was used to send the email — e.g. "PHP Mailer" or "Outlook."
SPF/DKIMSecurity checks. If an email fails these, it's likely "Spoofed" (Fake).
Return-PathWhere the email goes if it bounces. Scammers often forget to hide their real email here.
Investigation UseTracing a "Bomb Threat" email back to the original server.
How to read itIt looks like a wall of text. Press Ctrl+F and search for "Received" to find the IP chain.
